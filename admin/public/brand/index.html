For printing — shirts, banners, anything large

Print SVG
Vector · 3 solid colours
4000 px PNG
Transparent · if they cannot take SVG
Give the printer the Print SVG, not the ordinary logo. It is redrawn as three solid inks — green #004A23, gold #D69800 and white — with clean edges that stay sharp at any size. Screen printing uses solid inks anyway, so the gradient could not have been reproduced.

Which shirt colour

Cream or black work best; avoid dark green. On a dark green shirt the logo's own green disappears into the fabric and only the gold survives. Cream lets both the green and the gold read, and is the warmest match for the brand. Black gives the strongest contrast and makes the gold pop. White is the cheapest to print — two inks, no white underbase — but the white keyline around the mark becomes invisible.

SVG — vector, for screen use

Vector · transparent
The big PNGs are not sharper, only smoother. The original artwork is 439×410 pixels, so anything above that is upscaled. For anything printed large, use the SVG.
The SVG has flat gold instead of a gradient. No vector original exists, so this was traced from the image. It scales cleanly, but the gradient on the price tag became solid gold. If the designer still has the Illustrator or Figma file, export from there instead.
The white outline is part of the logo, not background. The mark has a white keyline around it and a hole through the bag handle. Cutting out every white pixel would have eaten the outline too, so only the background beyond the keyline is transparent.
